Denise Maria Zezell

OSA/OPTICA FELLOW since 2022. She coordinates a National Institute of Science and Technology of Radiation in Health Science (CNPq/INCT 406761/2022-1). She was elected for the board of the Brazilian Society for the Progress of Science (SBPC). Coordinator of postgraduate evaluation committee CNPq 35/2023 (National Council for Scientific and Technological Development). Bachelor in Physics (1984), MSc in Physics (1987) and PhD in Sciences from Universidade Estadual de Campinas (1991). Postdoc at the International Center for Theoretical Physics - Trieste, Italy (1992) and at the Center for Lasers and Applications at IPEN/CNEN-SP (1992-1995). She has been a full reserarcher at the Nuclear and Energy Research Institute of the Nuclear Energy National Commission (CNEN) since 1995, where she was Manager of the Lasers and Applications Center in 2008. From 2009 to 2013 she was the Coordinator of the Professional Master Program in Lasers in Dentistry at IPEN, together with School of Dentistry- University of Sao Paulo (USP). From 2013 to 2015 she was Vice-Coordinator of the Master's and Doctorate Program in Nuclear Technology at USP. She was principal investigator in a number of FAPESP (CEPID Optics and Photonics) and other National projects (CNPq INCT Photonics) a member of the Medical Physics Commission of the Brazilian Society of Physics from 2008-2014. She was Vice President of the Brazilian Association of Lasers in Dentistry (ABLO) 2010-2014. Participated in the Capes-CA Engineering II Quadrennial Evaluation Committee 2013-2016. She was the creator and since 2019 is the Dean of the Professional Master Course in Radiation Technology in Health Sciences, IPEN. Since 2017, she has been appointed OSA Traveling Lecturer, a member of Education Commission from CNEN. Member of the Deliberative Council of the Brazilian Photonics Society (2021-current) and its Newsletter editor for Biophotonics (2018-2019). During 2019 she was member of the Technical Group 9 of the Brazilian Committee for the Development of Nuclear Program. Since 2020 is member of the Committee of Photonics - Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation (MCTI). Work in the following fields: Physics, Biophotonics, in the study of Optical and Spectroscopic Properties of biological tissues mainly by micro-FTIR and fluorescence, infrared thermography, aiming at the development of new diagnostic and therapeutic processes for clinical applications of lasers in dentistry and medicine. She has been dedicated to the search for biomarkers to determine the effects of ionizing radiation on biological tissues as well as to study the early diagnosis of skin and breast tumors by vibrational spectroscopy (FTIR optical biopsy). https://linktr.ee/zezell (Texto informado pelo autor)

                              3. 2021-Atual. FAPESP 2021/00633-0 Processamento e Análise de Sinais Hiperespectrais Aplicados ao Diagnóstico Histopatológico
                                Descrição: A tipificação de anomalias em tecidos humanos, dentre elas as neoplasias, é realizada normalmente por meio de exames histológicos em lâminas contendo biópsias. As pesquisas em torno de imagens digitais de biópsia têm crescido com rapidez, alavancando o desenvolvimento e o aperfeiçoamento de métodos de processamento de imagens especialmente desenvolvidos ou adaptados para esta categoria de imagem. Os sinais hiperespectrais, obtidos por meio de equipamentos de espectroscopia no infravermelho, caracterizam-se por apresentar para cada pixel da imagem um espectro de valores de absorbância para as diferentes frequências, que se mostra sensível às características do tecido subjacente. O objetivo deste projeto é investigar e validar a hipótese de que os sinais hiperespectrais infravermelhos podem ser usados para o reconhecimento de padrões que permitam identificar regiões da lâmina contendo tecido sadio ou com características anormais, em especial a ocorrência de neoplasias. Para isto, bases de sinais hiperespectrais representando a absorbância em uma faixa de frequências de infravermelho (700-4000 cm -1 ), obtidos a partir de lâminas contendo amostras de tecido sadio e de tecido anormal oriundas de diferentes órgãos, a saber, tireoide, pele e mama, que serão analisadas com uso de técnicas computacionais de Aprendizagem Profunda. Esta técnica será empregada para a implementação de classificadores, com os quais será possível caracterizar as regiões da lâmina de acordo com as possíveis anomalias presentes. Com isto, o método proposto permitirá a análise automática de biópsias, de forma a tornar o processo de diagnóstico mais preciso e efetivo. Além disso, imagens obtidas a partir de lâminas de biópsia coradas com Hematoxilina e Eosina que são utilizadas para o diagnóstico patológico, também serão processadas por técnicas de visão computacional para comparação com os sinais hiperespectrais, visando estabelecer a possível detecção de padrões de anomalias que não são identificáveis pelo método visual correntemente utilizado..
